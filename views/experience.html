End User Experience

According to SpaceX, "Starlink is designed to deliver high speed broadband internet to locations where access has been unreliable, expensive, or completely unavailable." For some, the initial prices will likely be too much and not very competitive with existing options. However, with improved infrastructure and more customers, costs are likely to become more affordable internationally. Plans for pricing changes haven't been released, however Starlink may be competing with Amazon and with OneWeb in the U.K., which could drive prices down.1,3

A secretive beta program of Starlink has kicked off for an initial batch of users in the Northern US and Lower Canada, where the constellation is currently situated. Accessing the beta requires users to have a clear view of a certain potion of the sky, to sign a non-disclosure preventing discussion about their experience or taking photos of the setup, and to agree to regular feedback and testing. The beta involves no cost and is not immediately intended to replace existing internet service due to possible interruptions. However, the company indicated that the connection should be high-quality when connected.6

However, the initial full pilot will involve a $99 a month fee for users to access Starlink. This rollout is known currently as the "Better than Nothing Beta", as SpaceX is initially aiming for low expectations. The $500 initial setup package for installation includes a dish setup that can self-orient with a built in motor that connects to a power supply and router from Starlink. The user terminal included in this package is, according to Starlink, "more advanced than what's in fighter jets". Only a single cable connected the dish and the router as the system uses a power-over-Ethernet connection. Starlink app includes an augmented reality feature that helps users determine where to install the satellite dish.1,6

Expected speeds for users will range from 50-150 Mbps and connections will have latency from 20-40ms. There may be some lapses in connectivity at first, however SpaceX is working at upgrading and revamping their software, ground stations, and satellites to bring latency to 16-19ms and improve data speeds. The service should be much faster than existing satellite internet when all is said and done by overcoming existing infrastructure issues.1,3
